Nursing Simulation Website

Nursing Simulation

2020 brought a lot of new obstacles for humans across the world as COVID-19 turned our natural social atmospheres into isolated bubbles. While these new obstacles approached, students continued to complete their education virtually. However, students encountered limits with the essential real world experiences their career would bestow upon them. The lack of real world situations had become a concern within the nursing program students. In a normal world, they would experience real life situations and get hands on experience in the hospitals.

Myself and a group of Computer Science individuals took notice and felt the need to do something about it. We began to interview the nursing department faculty, as well as nursing students to discover the challenges that occured in their department. There needed to be a way in which students were able to gain real world experience through a virtual environment.

Through interviews and collaboration with the nursing department, we created a virtual simulation website that were to be used for clinical scenarios. This simulation was a “choose your own path” journey for undergraduate nursing students -- which involved multiple scenarios that they would find in the real world. Through this website, students were able to continue their studies in a virtual environment.

Through this experience, I developed the following skills:

  • Ruby on Rails Development
  • Backend and frontend Development
  • Cross disciplinary collaboration through interviews and meetings

    Applied Social/Cognitive Researcher

    The summer of 2020 brought a new birth of what was possible for virtual work. As the covid-19 pandemic hit across all parts of the world, humans quickly adapted to its circumstances.

    This new stage in the evolution of work brought the question of how virtual teams can effectively collaborate with one another via the computer screen. This question was introduced to me as I worked as an social/applied cognitive research assistant at Sandia National Laboratories under the supervision of Mike Heroux and Elaine Raybourn. We specifically looked at how software virtual teams could effectively collaborate.

    This project entailed gathering large ammounts of data and research that was related to software virtual teams. We used Github as our way to collect data. Through the use of Github, we created scholarly "templates" within the repository issues section. As we found interesting scholarly articles and findings, we would then post them onto our shared github repository. At the end of this experience, we created a central repository where individuals interested in understanding virtual collaboration could expand their knowledge.

    • Recidivism Algorithms

    Recidivism

    Predictive algorithms influence multiple aspects of life. For the most part, these predictive algorithms play a benefit in our society. However, there are cases in which these algorithms can hinder society. Recently, algorithms within the court system have introduced an ethical discussion on how prediction algorithms are used within high-risk scenarios. The Correctional Offender Management Profiling for Alternative Sanctions (COMPAS) is a prediction system aimed to predict recidivism. Recidivism is how likely an individual will re-engage in a crime.

    This project intended to break down the ethical, technical and social dilemma of recidivism algorithms within the court system. Further, it proceeded to analyze societies misconception and blind trust on complex algorithms. I look at the example of COMPAS and analyze its algorithm dataset. At the end, I provide future steps for recidivism prediction algorithms.

    Through this experience, I developed the following skills:

  • Conducting Interviews
  • Jupter Notebooks and Python
  • Extensive scientific research
  • How to present in front of a large crowd

    • Diverse Bodies, Beautiful Minds

    Diverse Bodies, Beautiful Minds

    Over the academic year of 2022-2023 I was a Co-Director for a nonprofit student run documentary team called Extending the Link(ETL). ETL’s project entailed the creation of an interactive film gallery. The interactive film gallery titled Diverse Bodies, Beautiful Minds: Voices of Disability Activism included insights and stories about Disability Activism. Through four short films, we showcased the voices of individuals that identify as disabled and non-disabled - establishing a narrative of authenticity and action. As a community, we joined together to learn through film and dialogue. While this gallery begins by highlighting the foundational goals of disability activism and key forms of resistance, it concludes with an exploration of intersectionality and allyship.

    Diverse Bodies, Beautiful Minds: Voices of Disability Activism, presents a non-traditional way of interacting and viewing film. One which uses physical space as a symbol of reflection that each member must take when presenting an individual's story and expertise. This film uses no alternative footage, but rather focuses on the footage of the interviewers as a means to emphasize the need to focus on the powerful insights each interviewer has to bring. Additionally, this film intends to find alternative ways in which we can incorporate accessibility through the use of captioning, audio captions, and ASL interpretations.

    Through this experience, I developed the following skills:

  • How to conduct interviews
  • How to film and edit documentary style films
  • How to manage a team of more than 30 individuals
  • How to cross collaborate with each team within our organization whom included marketing, videography, research, and outreach in order to work together to complete our goal
  • Organize an event space in which more than 150 people attended.
