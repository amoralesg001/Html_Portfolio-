Nursing Simulation Website

Nursing Simulation

2020 brought a lot of new obstacles for humans across the world as COVID-19 turned our natural social atmospheres into isolated bubbles. While these new obstacles approached, students continued to complete their education virtually. However, students encountered limites with the essential real world experiences their career would bestow upon them. The lack of real world situations had become a concern the nursing program students. In a normal world, they would experiencereal life situations and get hands on experience in the hospitals. However, COVID-19 made it impossible for them to gain those perspectives.

Myself and a group of Computer Science individuals took notice and felt the need to do something about it. We began to interview the nursing department faculty, as well as their students to discover the challenges that occured in their department. There needed to be a way in which students were able to gain real world experience through a virtual environment.

Through interviews and collaboration with the nursing department. We created a Virtual Simulation Website that were to be used for clinical scenarios. This simulation was a “choose your own path” journey for undergraduate nursing students -- which involved multiple scenarios that they would find in the real world. Through this website, students were able to continue their studies in a virtual environment.

Through this experience, I learned the following:

  • The ability to conduct interviews
  • Cross disciplinary collaboration
  • Front and back end coding through the design of Ruby on Rails (OOP)

    • Recidivism Algorithms

    Recidivism

    Predictive algorithms influence multiple aspects of life. For the most part, these predictive algorithms play a benefit in our society. However, there are cases in which these algorithms can hinder society. Recently, algorithms within the court system have introduced an ethical discussion on how prediction algorithms are used within high-risk scenarios. The Correctional Offender Management Profiling for Alternative Sanctions (COMPAS) is a prediction system aimed to predict recidivism. Recidivism is how likely an individual will re-engage in a crime.

    This project intended to break down the ethical, technical and social dilemma of recidivism algorithms within the court system. Further, it will proceeded to analyze societies misconception and blind trust on complex algorithms. I look at the example of COMPAS and analyze its algorithm dataset. At the end, I provide future steps for recidivism prediction algorithms.

    Throughout this project, I learned the following:

  • How to conduct interviews
  • How to conduct scientific research
  • How to display data through Jupyter notebooks and the use of the Python language
  • How to present in front of a large audience

    • Oleb Media Internship

    OLEB MEDIA

    The interactive film gallery titled Diverse Bodies, Beautiful Minds: Voices of Disability Activism will include insights and stories about this year's ETL theme, Disability Activism. Through four short films, we will showcase the voices of individuals that identify as disabled and non-disabled - establishing a narrative of authenticity and action. As a community, we will join together to learn through film and dialogue. While this gallery begins by highlighting the foundational goals of disability activism and key forms of resistance, it will conclude with an exploration of intersectionality and allyship.
    Diverse Bodies, Beautiful Minds: Voices of Disability Activism, presents a non-traditional way of interacting and viewing film. One which uses physical space as a symbol of reflection that each member must take when presenting an individual's story and expertise. This film uses no alternative footage, but rather focuses on the footage of the interviewers as a means to emphasize the need to focus on the powerful insights each interviewer has to bring. Additionally, this film intends to find alternative ways in which we can incorporate accessibility through the use of captioning, audio captions, and ASL interpretations.

    UX/UI Intern

    Applied Research Laboratories UX/UI Intern

    Over the summer of 2021 I had the opportunity to intern as a User Experience and User Interface developer at the Applied Research Laboratory at Penn State. Due to the confidentiality of this project, I am not able to go into specifics on the project. However, I can speak about the things I achieved during this internship and broadly speak about the project. The goal of this project was to help the end users navigate information within an email alerting system. After extensive interviews with the end users of the website, I was able to learn that navigating large amounts of important information embedded in the email was often difficult to navigate. Due to this finding, my project entailed making an automated HTML email which could highlight important phrases, and confidentiality levels of the information within the email.

    My main accomplishments during this project involved creating an automated HTML email that used a Natural Language Processor (NLP) to color code important information within the email. At the end of the internship, I was able to implement this new feature within the upcoming release of the website. We received positive feedback from the end users as they were able to easily navigate through large amounts of information that each email sent.

    Through this project, I learned the following:

  • Further skills and development in JavaScript, CSS, and HTML.
  • Development of automated HTML emails
  • The use of Natural Processing Languages
  • Conducting interviews on customers who used the website
  • Skills in User Experience and User Interface implementation
  • Cross collaboration between designers, developers, and customers
